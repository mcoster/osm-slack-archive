You may have seen a variety of procedures published for this type of C-H functionalisation. The good news is that the presence of a variety of procedures suggests that the reaction is tolerant to changes in how it is conducted. The bad news is that it can be hard to know where to start.

m.coster
2018-03-19 21:21:25
@xiaoyu.qian asked me about the conditions selected, with respect to which position on the pyrazine will react:

m.coster
2018-03-19 21:21:57

m.coster
2018-03-19 21:22:44
And very astutely pointed out that previous research has shown that pyridines react with different regioselectivity depending on the solvent system:

m.coster
2018-03-19 21:22:57
@m.coster uploaded a file: https://opensourcemalaria.slack.com/files/U8DBK598C/F9RGY1QBT/td9iwy2__je_ep5ep-u__im.png|TD9IWY2%_JE%EP5EP-U$$IM.png

m.coster
2018-03-19 21:26:10
There is an excellent and very detailed paper in J Am Chem Soc that discusses these selectivity effects - https://pubs.acs.org/doi/pdfplus/10.1021/ja406223k

m.coster
2018-03-19 21:28:20
Even for this paper, the triazolopyrazine we will be using as substrate is quite complex and regioselectivity is therefore difficult to predict.

m.coster
2018-03-19 21:29:05
One further comlicating factor is that I'm not sure how soluble the substrate is, in CH2Cl2 or CHCl3.

m.coster
2018-03-19 21:30:48
A/Prof. Rohan Davis and I recently applied the C-H functionalisation method with (CF3SO2)2Zn to a number of complex natural products, and we chose to do all reactions in DMSO since we weren't sure of solubility in many cases and DMSO is a much better solvent for many of these heteroaromatic compounds.

m.coster
2018-03-19 21:32:56
There is also the influence of acid. In general, this makes many of these reactions better when using electron rich heteroaromatics - when TFA is added, the ring nitrogen is protonated and it is probably this cationic species that reacts with the radical.

m.coster
2018-03-19 21:36:26

Team, when you do this procedure, please seek advice from @pakornwit about the workup when the reaction is complete. This needs to be scaled appropriately. A good rule of thumb is to add aqueous component of ~1x to 3x volume of your reaction volume, then extract with 3x organic solvent, such that the total organic solvent volume is about the same as the aqueous. On a small scale, it is often impractical to deal with volumes less than ~5-10 ml for the work up.
